Fall in Love with Bayfield Peninsula

Fall is by far my favorite season in Wisconsin. The beautiful colors, brisk air, and winter vegetables are some of my favorite aspects of fall. In 2019, the cartography "twittersphere" was flooded with stunning fall maps. Naturally, I had to take a stab at making a fall map!

I learned how to work my way around photoshop making this reference map. I enjoyed doing a lot of manipulation with highlights, shadows, textures, and other effects. I love how the textures for the forests turned out. Also, I used an effect called Satin which randomly adds a color overlay to certain parts of a feature group. This helped a lot with representing the tendency of forests to have patches of trees with changed colors and non-changed colors in fall.

Created using Photoshop and Arc Hillshade
